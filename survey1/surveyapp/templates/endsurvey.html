This survey was concerned with an Artificial Intelligence technology which can create images of faces of people that don't exist.

Participants in this study received one of the following four forms of assistance to help researchers understand how potential assistance methods affect performance at the survey's central task:

  • No assistance
    This group were asked to rank a set of greeting messages and to add their own version, before the survey's central task began; and then to do the same for a series of thank you messages.
    This task was given to participants who are in the control group.
    It was a "filler" activity which took approximately as long as the activities the other groups were asked to complete, while having neutral effect on participants' performance on the task.

  • Familiarization before questions
    This group were shown twenty examples of the AI-generated images before the survey's central task began.

  • Advice before questions
    This group were given three categories of "tell-tale features", which may have helped to distinguish the AI-generated images from the images of real human faces.

  • Advice before questions with reminder during questions
    This group were given the same three categories of "tell-tale features" as the "Advice before questions" group.
    Additionally, they were given a reminder of what these categories' "tell-tale features" were at the top of each webpage for the duration of the central task.
